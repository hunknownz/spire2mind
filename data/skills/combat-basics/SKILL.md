Combat priorities for autonomous play:

- Spend all available energy each player turn. Wasted energy is wasted tempo.
- Prefer lines that improve survival and preserve tempo over flashy but low-value plays.
- When lethal is impossible, prioritize block against large incoming damage.
- If incoming damage is near-lethal or would leave the player in a death spiral, block first and only add damage after survival is covered.
- At low health, treat "take less damage this turn" as more important than speculative setup or awkward value plays.
- If a card requires a target, prefer the enemy with the highest impact intent or the cleanest kill setup.
- Free or cheap damage is good, but not if it crowds out the only realistic defensive line.
- Against sleeping or harmless enemy turns (buff intents), convert spare energy into damage or setup instead of panic-blocking.
- When a single enemy is close to death, favor lines that shorten the fight and reduce future incoming damage.
- Against multiple enemies, evaluate AoE cards higher — clearing weak enemies reduces total incoming damage faster than single-target focus.
- Do not end turn while a clearly playable high-value card remains, unless holding it is strongly justified.
- Play draw and 0-cost cards early in the turn to reveal more options before committing energy.
- In uncertain states, choose the safest legal line that keeps the fight moving.
- Track enemy intent patterns: enemies that buff this turn will hit harder next turn. Use safe turns to set up.
