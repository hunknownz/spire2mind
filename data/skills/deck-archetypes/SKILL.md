Deck-building reminders:

- Favor cards and relics that reinforce what the current deck already does well.
- Avoid bloating the deck with mediocre pickups when skip is acceptable. A 15-card deck draws key cards twice as often as a 30-card deck.
- Card removal is especially valuable when the deck is still small or clogged with weak starters (Strikes, Defends).
- Expensive cards need a believable way to be cast; cheap consistency cards are often stronger than speculative greed.
- When choosing between similar rewards, prefer reliability and immediate impact over narrow upside.
- In early floors (1-6), premium commons like efficient block, card draw, and clean damage usually beat niche payoff cards.
- Do not overvalue a card just because it costs 0; conditional or low-floor cards should still lose to strong commons that immediately stabilize the deck.
- After a rough or low-health run, bias toward survivability, draw, and removal over speculative synergies.
- Track the attack/defense balance: a deck that is all damage and no block will die to hard-hitting enemies; a deck that is all block and no scaling will die to long fights.
- Upgrades matter: an upgraded Defend or Strike is meaningfully better. Prioritize upgrading high-frequency cards.
